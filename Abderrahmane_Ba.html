Mauritania


Mauritania (in Arabic: موريتانيا, romanized: Mūrītāniyā),
officially the Islamic Republic of Mauritania,
is a country located in the Maghreb region of North Africa.
Its area is 1,030,700 km², making it the 29th largest country in the world.
Most of its territory is covered by the Sahara Desert.

The country has a coastline of about 800 km on the Atlantic Ocean,
stretching from Ndiago in the south to Nouadhibou in the north.
It shares borders with Algeria (north-northeast),
Mali (east and south-southeast), Senegal (southwest),
and Western Sahara to the north.

The capital and largest city is Nouakchott,
and other major cities include Nouadhibou,
Kiffa, Kaédi, Néma and Rosso.

History

The territory of Mauritania became a French colony in 1903.
The country gained its independence in 1960. Since then,
Mauritania has experienced several coups and periods of military rule.
In 2019, Mohamed Ould Ghazouani won the presidential election,
marking the first peaceful transfer of power since independence.

Geography

Mauritania is located at the crossroads between North Africa and Sub-Saharan Africa,
making it a zone of cultural and geographical contact.
The country is mostly desert, with vast areas of sand and semi-arid zones.

Population and Society

In 2023, the population of Mauritania is estimated at 4,244,878 inhabitants,
ranking the country 128th in the world. The official language is Arabic,
but French is also widely used in administration and education.
Sunni Islam is the state religion and represents more than 99% of the population.

Organization and International Relations

Mauritania is a member of several international organizations, such as:

  • the African Union (AU)
  • the Arab League
  • the Arab Maghreb Union (AMU)
  • the Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC)
  • the International Organization of La Francophonie (OIF)
